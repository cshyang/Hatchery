# Naming & Positioning Brief

**Date:** 2026-06-03
**Status:** Draft for review
**Trigger:** Is "Hatchery / Hetchery" the right name? It names the *birth* of an agent, not the persistent value.

## The real outcome of this session: positioning, not just a name

Naming kept failing because the *story* kept moving (teammate → do-the-work → instant-spawn).
You can't name a product whose soul isn't fixed. We fixed it.

### The settled soul

**Internal north star (the how):**
> Your team's nervous system — it senses every conversation, connects the signals that
> matter, and **learns its reflexes**: the right response gets faster and more automatic
> the more it runs.

**Promise to a human (the what they buy):**
> Nothing falls through the cracks — and the right move fires on its own, sharper every week.

Maps onto the existing architecture:
- ambient ingestion → *senses everything*
- cross-thread connect → *routes & associates signals*
- proactive review / earn-the-turn → *fires a considered response*
- skill self-improvement / self-evolution → *pathways harden into reflexes*

This is the differentiated wedge. "AI teammate," "does the work," and "instant spawn" are
all commodity claims owned by funded incumbents. "Ambient sensing that **learns reflexes**"
is the thing the specs actually pour care into, and the thing competitors don't lead with.

## Empirical naming finding

~56 candidates scanned across 8 batches (team-words, craft-words, worker-words,
spawn/summon words, reflex/instinct words). Result: **in 2026 AI naming, "distinctive +
available + real dictionary word" is impossible.** Every distinctive real word on every
shelf is taken, usually by a funded AI company. The dictionary lane is exhausted.

→ The only path to distinctive **and** ownable is to **coin** a word from the soul.

## Finalists

| Name | Source / meaning | Read | Verdict |
|------|------------------|------|---------|
| **Myla** | from *myelin* — the sheath that hardens neural pathways with use | warm, brandable, ties directly to "hardens / learns reflexes" | **Recommended.** Risk: can read as an AI-assistant person-name; may undercut "serious infrastructure." |
| **Sentir** | Sp./Fr. *to sense / to feel* | elegant, clean | Caution: it's a generic common verb in two languages — the **same flaw that killed Obra**. Likely fails your own "no generic foreign noun" rule. |
| **Troupe** | the cast you direct | clean on every axis, distinctive, shippable today | Fits the *older* teammate-crew framing; weaker tie to the nervous-system soul. The "ship now" fallback. |

All coined/clean names still require a **USPTO + domain check** before commitment.

## Competitive flag (do this before carving a name)

The "learns-from-use / tacit-knowledge" thesis has **two funded twins**:
- **Interloom** — $16.5M (Mar 2026), "unlocking *tacit knowledge* for AI agents."
- **Engram Lab / Engram AI** — "models that learn from use, update in seconds."

Funded competition validates the wedge but means you're not alone in it. Pull both apart
and sharpen the difference before finalizing positioning or name.
