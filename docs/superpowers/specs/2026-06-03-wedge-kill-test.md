# Wedge Kill-Test — "The Teammate With Taste"

**Date:** 2026-06-03
**Status:** Ready to run
**Purpose:** Cheaply prove or bury the wedge BEFORE naming or further building.

## The one hypothesis

> Teams with the dropped-balls-in-channels pain value **restraint** enough to keep a quiet
> teammate. Bot **noise-fatigue** is real, and *"knows when to shut up"* is the differentiator —
> not "it learns" (commodity, owned by the platform).

Everything else (build, name, scale) is downstream. If this is false, none of it matters.

## Segment: founder / small-startup teams

Chosen for **reachability this week**. Honest caveat — this is the *mild-pain* end, so the
inference is asymmetric:

- A **positive here is strong** (if even mild pain pulls, sharp pain will love it).
- A **negative here is a yellow flag, not a death certificate** — re-test ONE sharp-pain
  channel (incident/support or an agency's client channel) before truly walking away.
- Biggest risk: **peer politeness** (founders being nice to a founder). Discount all praise.
  Count only behavior.

## Pre-committed scorecard (decide now, before you have feelings)

```
PROCEED   noise-fatigue is visceral in interviews  AND  they PULL in the live test
YELLOW    founders shrug  → run one sharp-pain channel before deciding
KILL      shrug among founders  AND  shrug in one sharp-pain channel
```

**The only signal that counts: PULL, not politeness.** Pull = they act on it, reach for it,
or miss it when it's gone.

## Stage A — Discovery (this week, no build)

**Recruiting message** (send to ~8 founder friends):
> "Running a 2-week experiment on how teams keep track of things in Slack/Discord. Two asks,
> both free: (1) 20 min to hear how your team chat actually works; (2) let me lurk in your
> busiest channel for 2 weeks and occasionally flag stuff that looks dropped — keep what's
> useful, ignore the rest. In?"

**Interview questions** (*The Mom Test* — past behavior only, NEVER pitch the idea):
1. Walk me through the last time a decision or task got lost in your team chat.
2. How do you track who said they'd do what? Where does that break?
3. What bots/integrations are in your main channel? Which have you muted or removed — why?
4. When you're heads-down for a day, what falls through?
5. *(if they start praising the idea)* → redirect: "When did that last actually happen to you?"

**Kill read:** if <half can recall a specific dropped-ball story unprompted AND can't name a
bot they muted for noise → the noise-pain is imaginary. Stop here.

## Stage B — Wizard-of-Oz (2 weeks, ONE channel, zero AI)

You **are** the restrained teammate, by hand. The cheapest way to test whether *perfect taste*
is valuable is to supply it with a human and watch. (Salvaged from "do things that don't scale.")

**Rules:**
- **Max one** high-signal note per day. If nothing's worth saying, say nothing — *that is the product.*
- Never explain the experiment in-channel.

**Intervention types that land for founders:**
- Dropped commitment: *"X said they'd own Y on Tuesday — still open?"*
- Contradiction / decision: *"This cuts against the pricing call last week."*
- Cross-thread connect: *"Same issue as #bugs on Thursday."*

**Instrumented silence-probe (the cleanest pull test):** on days 8–10, go *completely silent.*
Log whether anyone notices or asks where you went.

**Log** (one row per intervention):

| date | what you flagged | reaction (none / ack / acted) | referenced later? |
|------|------------------|-------------------------------|-------------------|
|      |                  |                               |                   |

Silence-probe result: did anyone notice you went quiet? (Y/N — who, what they said)

## Reading the result

- **Pull (proceed):** they act on notes, @ you expecting help, or complain when you go silent.
- **Politeness (discount):** "cool," a thumbs-up, then nothing.
- **Null (yellow → test a sharp segment):** notes ignored, silence unnoticed.

## Cost

~3 weeks · ~0 build · a few hours of your time. Cheapest possible way to find out if the
idea deserves a name.
